INTERNAL QUALITY CONTROL

  1. PURPOSE
    1. This document contains the general protocols for assuring the quality of examination procedures through an internal quality control system.
    2. The quality control system will monitor analytical processes, detect errors, and help prevent the reporting of incorrect results.
    3. As part of the quality assurance program, an internal quality control system is one of the methods employed by PHDB1 Laboratory to verify that results meet the intended standards of quality.
  2. COMPUTER PROGRAMS
    1. Microsoft
    2. PCS Labcare
  3. RESPONSIBILITIES
    1. Laboratory Director
    2. All the staff trained and authorized in the Department of Biochemistry.
      1. Technologist:
        1. Responsible for the performance of QC procedures and troubleshooting errors.
        2. Responsible for the daily review of QC results through LIS-LJ chart
        3. Responsible for documentation of all QC errors and the corrective actions performed.
      2. Senior Technologist:
        1. Responsible for the daily review of QC results, and will ensure that quality control errors are investigated and corrected.
        2. Responsible for making sure that no patient results are released unless the QC results for those tests meet the acceptable criteria.
        3. Responsible for monthly review of imprecision, measurement of uncertainties, external control results and validations
      3. Quality Control Officer:
        1. Responsible for the periodic review of the internal quality control system.
        2. Responsible for monitoring and informing validation schedules.
